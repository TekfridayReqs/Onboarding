    01 Stage One

    Know Your Company

    Who we are. TekFriday Processing Solution is a fintech company whose mission is to build innovative solutions that help financial services companies based in the US grow and compete in this fast-paced world.

    Our Origin Story

    In an overcrowded alternative-lending market where conformity was the norm, three individuals set out to build a disruptive force — to sway away from that conformity and become a pioneer. Anil, Srinath and Mridula saw a gap in the industry and, using their collective ideation and ingenuity, built solutions that help alternative-lending entities become leaders in their own segment. We started operations in 2013 with fewer than 20 members; today we have grown into a team of more than 200. In 2019, TekFriday was acquired by Advance Financials and became a captive delivery centre.

    How We're Different

    We are a lean team that works in an agile set-up, thriving in fast-paced environments to deliver every one of these services with speed and focus.

    Our Goal

    To always look to innovate and build solutions that help us grow, while aligning with our business strategy — redefining financial solutions with cutting-edge technology.

    Our Values

    Innovative

    We look at a problem or process and seek better ways to work, while creating value to help grow the business. Not superficially using words like "automation" or "AI" — but real changes and optimizations that make a process work better or create more value.

    Adaptive

    Embrace change while remaining agile. Because we always operate in a lean and agile way, we don't expect employees to stay rigid about "roles and responsibilities" — we expect everyone to embrace change and work toward a common, collective goal.

    Impact

    Focus on business outcomes that create meaningful value while aligning with company strategy. Everyone has a role to play in getting us where we want to be, so reframe how your work and actions create impact on the business.

    Transparency & Compliance

    Be respectful, honest and open. Unlike traditional organizations, we work in a matrix model where we collaborate across departments to drive business excellence — keeping egos aside, working as a collective unit, and staying open to feedback.

    02 Stage Two

    Know Your Culture

    "The only way to do great work is to love what you do."— Steve Jobs

    Our work culture is defined by six everyday behaviours. Together they shape how decisions are made, how we collaborate, and how success is achieved.

    Ownership

    Whatever task is allotted to you, you are the owner — the sole torchbearer responsible for completing it to the best of your ability.

    Accountability

    Finishing a task is admirable — but is it your best work? Always aim for the best possible outcome.

    Collaboration

    In our matrix hierarchy we keep communication open and work collectively toward the common goal — avoiding bureaucratic red tape. Work should be seamless, without the hurdles of unwarranted approvals.

    Continuous Learning

    Learn skills or technologies that improve our systems or approach. Every employee has the potential to exceed today's limits and become an asset — and it isn't restricted to your current work.

    Strategy Focus

    As a captive delivery unit for a US lending company, our goals align with our parent's: to be the best in the industry while remaining a disruptive innovator. Every decision and action should align with this.

    Respect

    An inclusive ecosystem that values every insight and idea. There are no bad ideas. We come from different backgrounds and aspirations and respect each other's opinions and ways of life — whoever you are, if you live our values and respect one another, you'll fare well.

    03 Stage Three

    Know Your Team

    Because we work in a matrix structure, understanding how the team connects — and what's expected of you — is key to moving fast without friction.

    Team Orientation

    Here you'll get to know the organization and reporting structure:

    • Where you sit within the organization chart.
    • What a line manager and a functional manager are, and how they differ.
    • Who your reporting manager is — and who else can assign you work apart from them.
    • Who your key stakeholders are: the people you'll work and collaborate with.
    • Your cross-functional dependencies — the stakeholders and departments you'll work with.

    Team Expectations

    Four things we count on from everyone:

    • Communication — communicate, be proactive, and update regularly.
    • Collaboration — support one another and share knowledge or skills that help streamline the work.
    • Problem Solving — focus on solutions, not problems; when an issue arises, ensure that kind of problem never comes up again.
    • Professionalism — integrity in personal and professional conduct; always be reliable and respect each other.

    8.1 HR Policies

    Office Locations & Timings

    How and when we work. Your exact timings depend on the project, department and office location you're assigned to.

    Office Locations & Timings

    We operate from 10:00 AM to 7:00 PM, though timings depend on the project and department you're assigned to. We work from two locations — One West Terminus and MJR Magnifique — and the timings for these two differ.

    Mandatory Hours

    Complete 9 hours a day based on your project and work location. Not completing your hours on more than 3 instances results in loss of pay. You must also log in on time — a grace period is allowed, but more than 3 late logins also results in loss of pay.

    For full details, read the Attendance Policy on HRMS or the employee handbook.

    8.3 HR Policies

    Leave Policy

    Our leave cycle runs from April to March. Only earned leaves are eligible for carry-forward or encashment, up to a maximum Earned Leave balance of 60 days. In total, employees have 27 days of leave.

    1 Your 27 Days — Breakdown & Accrual
    Leave TypeDaysAccrualCarry Forward
    Earned Leave15Accrued monthly; pro-rata for new joinersUp to 60 days
    Casual Leave6Accrued monthlyCarried to next quarter
    Sick Leave62 accrued per quarterSee note below
    Sick Leave detail: 2 sick leaves are accrued each quarter. In the last quarter, if your remaining balance is below 5 you receive 2 days; if it is more than 4 and less than 6 you receive the remaining balance; and if you have already exhausted all leaves in earlier quarters, your last-quarter balance is zero. Availing more than one sick leave at a time requires a doctor's note.
    Encashment: encashing Earned Leave at year-end is optional — the default setting is carry-forward. Only Earned Leave is eligible for carry-forward or encashment.
